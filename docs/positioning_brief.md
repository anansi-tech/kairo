# Kairo Positioning Brief

**Version 2.0 — May 2026**

## Who Kairo is for

Working professionals aged 30-60 — directors, managers, senior individual contributors — with corporate or personal budget for professional development. They've heard about AI everywhere: their company is "adopting AI," junior employees are using it, peers are losing jobs to it, and they're privately panicking that they're falling behind. They've maybe opened ChatGPT once. They couldn't tell you what an agent is. They don't want to read another _Harvard Business Review_ think piece. They want to **leave a workshop knowing how to actually use AI in their job**.

Secondary audience: companies wanting in-house AI fluency training for entire teams or departments (B2B engagements, $30K-$50K per session, 15-20 participants per cohort).

The buyer is _not_ the executive making AI strategy decisions (different product, different price tier) and _not_ the engineer building production systems (different product, different curriculum). Kairo's buyer is the vast middle layer of corporate America that has been told AI is coming for their job and hasn't been shown what to do about it.

---

## What Kairo is

A 2-day in-person hands-on workshop in Miami where 15 working professionals open their laptops and learn to actually use AI in their real jobs. Taught by David Noel — Anansi founder, CS adjunct at Nova Southeastern, PhD dissertation committee member, production AI/blockchain systems builder. $3,000 per seat.

Day 1: What AI actually is (live demos, no slides). Hands-on with ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini against participants' real work. Prompt engineering that produces useful outputs. First working automation built by end of day.

Day 2: Connecting AI to other tools (Zapier, n8n, Make). Building simple agents for participants' job context. What to trust AI with and what not to. How to scale what they built. How to bring it back to their team.

Outcome: every participant leaves with at least 2-3 working AI integrations they'll use in their actual job on Monday morning.

Two adjacent formats:

- **Virtual** — same 2-day curriculum delivered as two 4-hour sessions one week apart over Zoom. 20 seats per cohort (larger than in-person because instructor doesn't need to walk the room). $1,500/seat. Humane pacing protects momentum without consuming a full week of calendars. Same content, adapted for remote delivery.
- **In-company corporate** — private delivery, 1-2 days onsite at the client, 15-20 participants. $30,000-$50,000 per engagement depending on customization. Same content, tailored to the company's actual tools and workflows.

Class size: **15 seats in-person, 20 seats virtual.** In-person caps lower to enable walk-the-room help; virtual scales higher because instructor stays at the keyboard. First 3 cohorts of each format used to learn the rhythm before scaling either.

Year-1 target: 8-12 cohorts total, blended across formats. Cap at 12 to protect Spice as primary focus.

## What makes Kairo different (the moat)

**Operator-taught, not theory-taught.** Most AI fluency training is delivered by people who _teach_ AI for a living. Kairo is taught by someone who _builds_ AI systems in production. When Kairo teaches "use Claude to draft this report," the instructor uses Claude to ship real code daily. When Kairo teaches "build an agent for your inbox," the instructor has built production agents for Anansi's products. The depth shows.

**Hands-on, not lecture.** Day 1 hour 1: laptops open. Every concept is followed by 15-20 minutes of "now try it in your context." Participants leave with working AI in their actual job, not notes about working AI in someone else's case study.

**Anansi as the proof.** Anansi is an AI-first technology company. Spice is a live production system on Sui. CaribCoin is real cryptography. The instructor doesn't just talk about prompting and agents — he uses them daily to build real software that real people use. Most AI trainers are educators who studied AI. Kairo's instructor is a builder who teaches.

**Academic credibility without academic distance.** Adjunct CS professor at Nova Southeastern University, PhD dissertation committee member. Real teaching, real students, real research oversight. The "Kairo by Anansi" branding signals serious — Institute language reserved for when there's substance behind it.

**Pricing positioned at the top of boutique tier.** $3,000/seat in-person sits at the top of the $1,500-$3,000/day market range for hands-on AI fluency workshops (DataTrainers, Improving, similar). Most boutique trainers at this price point are individual practitioners. Kairo charges the same while offering Anansi-CEO-plus-CS-adjunct-plus-PhD-committee credentials — meaning the buyer feels they're getting a bargain on credentials, not paying a premium. That's the right side of the value equation to be on.

## Market context (May 2026)

- **University-branded AI programs**: $2,500–$50,000/seat, 3-day to 6-month formats. (Wharton, MIT xPRO, Berkeley, Columbia, Kellogg, Harvard, INSEAD).
- **Independent/boutique workshops**: $1,000–$3,000/day per person in-person; hybrid programs $800–$2,500/person.
- **Corporate B2B**: $5,000–$15,000/participant for private delivery; full engagements $12,000–$250,000.
- **2026 trends driving demand**: (1) Operationalization over experimentation — companies done with pilots, demanding ROI. (2) AI literacy now table stakes for executive roles. (3) "Change fitness" as a core leadership capability (HBS framing). (4) Top-down enterprise AI programs replacing crowdsourced adoption (PwC 2026). (5) Independent operators with verifiable credentials commanding premium rates because vendor-captive consultants are seen as biased.
- **What's not working**: Generic AI tools training. Theory-heavy MBA-style programs. Certificate mills. Consultants who teach but don't build.

Kairo sits in the hands-on AI fluency workshop tier (Type 2 in the standard four-segment model — operator skills, not executive strategy or basic literacy). Most workshops at this price point are taught by individual practitioners or small training shops without serious credentials. The builder-plus-academic profile is the differentiator.
